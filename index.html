Famous Attractions

Great Wall

A world-famous ancient defense project, stretching thousands of miles, and a symbol of China.

The Forbidden City

Located in Beijing, this imperial palace boasts rich history and culture. It is the largest and best-preserved wooden palace complex in the world.

Guilin Scenery

Famous for its green mountains, clear waters, unique caves, and beautiful rocks. Known as 'the best landscape under heaven'.

West Lake

Located in Hangzhou, renowned for its beautiful scenery and rich cultural heritage.

Local Cuisine

Peking Duck

Crispy outside and tender inside, Peking Duck is one of Beijing's most iconic dishes.

Chongqing Hotpot

Spicy and flavorful with a variety of ingredients, a signature food experience in southern China.

Shanghai Soup Dumplings

Thin skin, juicy filling, a traditional delicacy from the Jiangnan region.

Lanzhou Noodles

Chewy noodles in a savory broth, a representative noodle dish from northwest China.

Travel Tips

  • Best Time to Visit: Spring and autumn offer pleasant weather for most regions.
  • Transportation: China has a well-developed high-speed rail and air network, making intercity travel convenient.
  • Visa: Most foreign visitors need to apply for a Chinese visa in advance.
  • Currency: Renminbi (CNY). Mobile payments (Alipay, WeChat Pay) are widely used.
  • Language: Mandarin is the official language. Major attractions have English signs.
