Xiamen

A charming coastal city with colonial heritage and Gulangyu Island.

Xiamen (Amoy), a coastal city in Fujian province, is one of China's most pleasant cities to visit. It combines colonial-era architecture, a vibrant food scene, easy access to beaches, and the charming car-free island of Gulangyu across the harbor.

The city has a relaxed, subtropical atmosphere with tree-lined streets, seaside promenades, and a strong blend of Fujian, Taiwanese, and international influences.

Gulangyu Island

Gulangyu (鼓浪屿, meaning "Drum Wave Island") is a UNESCO World Heritage Site just across the harbor from Xiamen. The island is famous for its colonial architecture, car-free streets, piano museum, and seaside atmosphere. Take the ferry from Dongshan Port (10 min crossing, tickets ~35 yuan); visitor numbers are capped daily so book ahead on weekends.

Highlights: Piano Museum, Shuzhuang Garden, Sunlight Rock (日光岩) for 360-degree views.

Nanputuo Temple

Nanputuo Temple (南普陀寺) is one of Xiamen's most important Buddhist temples, located at the foot of Five Elders Mountain and adjacent to Xiamen University. Known for its vegetarian restaurant and hiking trails with views over the campus.

Zengcuo'an Village

Zengcuo'an (曾厝垵) is a lively pedestrian village with boutique hotels, cafes, local snacks, and artisan shops. Once a fishing village, now Xiamen's most colorful neighborhood -- great for evening strolls and local Fujian snacks.

How to get there

By air: Xiamen Gaoqi International Airport (XMN) has extensive domestic routes and international connections to Southeast Asia, Japan, and Korea. Metro and buses connect to the city center (~30 min).

By train: Xiamen Railway Station and Xiamen North Railway Station serve high-speed trains. Shanghai (~4h), Fuzhou (~1.5h), Shenzhen (~3h).

Best time to visit

Autumn and winter (Oct-Mar) offer the best weather -- warm and dry. Spring (Apr-May) is also pleasant. Avoid the typhoon season (Jul-Sep).

Getting around

  • Metro / BRT: Bus Rapid Transit and metro lines connect most tourist areas.
  • Didi / Taxi: Very convenient for short trips across the city.
  • Ferry to Gulangyu: Book tickets via the "屿见" app or at Dongshan Port. Round trip ~35 yuan; ferries run 07:10-21:00.

Suggested itineraries

1 day: Nanputuo Temple (morning) -- Xiamen University + campus walk -- Zengcuo'an Village (evening).

3 days: Day 1: Gulangyu Island (full day); Day 2: Nanputuo Temple + Huli Mountain Fortress + Zengcuo'an; Day 3: Beach day + Xiamen food tour.

Practical tips

  • Gulangyu tickets: Book ferry tickets in advance via the app, especially on weekends.
  • Food: Try Xiamen classics: oyster omelette (海蛎煎), sesame rice balls (麻糍), and fish ball soup (鱼丸汤).
  • Language: Xiamen dialect (Minnan) is different from Mandarin, but locals speak standard Mandarin.
