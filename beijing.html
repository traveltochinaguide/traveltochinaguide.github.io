About Beijing

Beijing, the capital of China, is a city where ancient traditions meet modern life. With a history spanning over 3,000 years, Beijing is home to some of the world's most iconic landmarks, including the Great Wall and the Forbidden City. Today, it is a vibrant metropolis, a political and cultural center, and a gateway to exploring Chinese heritage.

Top Attractions

Great Wall of China

The Great Wall, stretching over 13,000 miles, is one of the greatest wonders of the world. The Badaling and Mutianyu sections near Beijing are the most popular for visitors.

The Forbidden City

Once the imperial palace of the Ming and Qing dynasties, the Forbidden City is the largest ancient palatial structure in the world, featuring over 9,000 rooms and magnificent architecture.

Temple of Heaven

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Temple of Heaven is where emperors prayed for good harvests. Its circular buildings and vast park are a masterpiece of Chinese architecture.

Summer Palace

Known for its beautiful gardens, lakes, and pavilions, the Summer Palace was the royal family's summer retreat and is now a peaceful escape from the city bustle.

Local Cuisine

Peking Duck

Beijing's signature dish, famous for its crispy skin and tender meat, usually served with pancakes, scallions, and sweet bean sauce.

Zhajiangmian (Soybean Paste Noodles)

A classic Beijing noodle dish with thick wheat noodles topped with savory soybean paste, minced pork, and fresh vegetables.

Jianbing (Chinese Crepe)

A popular street breakfast, Jianbing is a crispy crepe filled with egg, scallions, cilantro, and savory sauces.

Donkey Burger

A unique Beijing snack, this sandwich features tender donkey meat in a crispy bun, often enjoyed with green peppers.

Travel Tips

  • Best Time to Visit: Spring (April–May) and autumn (September–October) offer pleasant weather and clear skies.
  • Transportation: Beijing has an extensive subway system and convenient public transport. Taxis and ride-hailing apps are widely available.
  • Language: Mandarin Chinese is the official language. English is spoken at major hotels and tourist sites.
  • Currency: Renminbi (CNY). Mobile payments (Alipay, WeChat Pay) are widely used.
  • Visa: Most visitors need a Chinese visa. Some nationalities can enjoy 144-hour visa-free transit.
