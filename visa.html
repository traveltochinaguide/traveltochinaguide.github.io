China Visa & Entry Requirements

Plan your trip with confidence — up-to-date visa policies and practical guidance

Visa-Free Entry for 2026

China has significantly expanded its visa-free policy in recent years, allowing citizens of many countries to visit without a visa for tourism purposes. However, policies change frequently — always verify with your local Chinese embassy before departure.

Visa-Free Countries

The following countries' citizens can enter China without a visa for short stays. Duration and conditions vary by nationality.

🇪🇺

⚠️ Note: Visa-free durations are measured in days of stay, not calendar days from entry. Multiple entries within the validity period may be allowed.

Countries Requiring a Visa

Citizens of most other countries need to obtain a visa before traveling to China. The most common visa types are listed below.

Common Visa Types

Tourist Visa (L)

For leisure travel, family visits, and tourism. Usually single or multiple entry, valid for 30-90 days per stay.

Business Visa (M)

For short-term business activities, trade fairs, and commercial exchanges. Similar duration to L visa.

Transit Visa (F)

For academic exchanges, scientific cooperation, and non-commercial visits. Often requires an invitation letter.

Q2 Family Visit Visa

For visits to Chinese citizens or residents who are immediate family. Longer validity and multiple entry options available.

Document Requirements

📘

Valid passport (6+ months validity, 2 blank pages)

📷

Recent passport-size photo (white background)

📝

Completed visa application form (Form V.2013)

✈️

Round-trip flight itinerary and hotel reservations

💼

Proof of financial means or invitation letter

💰

Visa fee (varies by nationality and processing speed)

How to Apply

1

Check Requirements

Determine which visa type you need and confirm your eligibility based on your nationality and purpose of visit.

2

Gather Documents

Prepare your passport, application form, photo, itinerary, and any required supporting documents.

3

Submit Application

Submit your application in person at the nearest Chinese embassy or consulate. Some locations require appointments.

4

Wait for Processing

Standard processing takes 4 business days. Rush and super-rush options are available for additional fees.

5

Collect Your Visa

Pick up your passport with the visa stamped inside. Verify all details (name, dates, number of entries) before traveling.

Essential Travel Tips

Check Before You Go

Visa policies change frequently. Check the latest requirements with your local Chinese Embassy or official government sources 2-3 weeks before departure.

Transit Exemptions

Citizens of many countries can transit through Chinese airports without a visa if staying within 24-144 hours and not leaving the airport zone. Check your specific transit conditions.

Hong Kong & Macau

Hong Kong and Macau have separate immigration policies. Many nationalities can visit these regions visa-free for short stays. Check SAR-specific requirements.

Tibet Permit

Foreign visitors to Tibet need both a Chinese visa and a Tibet Travel Permit (issued by the Tibet Tourism Bureau). Group tours are usually required for Tibet visits.

Payment in China

Mobile payments like Alipay and WeChat Pay are dominant in China. Major credit cards are accepted in hotels and large stores.
