I Ching

I Ching (iching) — an introduction to the Book of Changes, hexagrams, history and practical uses. Learn about iching divination and modern interpretation.

What is the I Ching (iching)?

The I Ching (also written iching, Chinese: 易经) — literally the Book of Changes — is one of the oldest Chinese classics. It combines cosmology, philosophy and a system of 64 hexagrams used historically for divination and decision-making.

History & structure

The iching developed over millennia. Each of its 64 hexagrams is formed from six lines (broken or unbroken). Traditional commentaries — the Ten Wings — expanded the text into a rich philosophical work used by scholars and sages.

How iching divination works

Modern iching methods use coins or yarrow stalk techniques to generate a hexagram. Interpreting the iching hexagram involves reading its meaning, line transformations, and how it relates to your question.

Why read the I Ching today?

  • Philosophical insight: iching offers frameworks for change and decision-making.
  • Cultural context: Understanding 易经 deepens appreciation for Chinese thought, history and literature.
  • Practical uses: meditation, creative prompts, and reflective decision processes.

Further reading & resources

Recommended translations and commentaries include works by Richard Wilhelm (translated by Cary F. Baynes), and modern commentaries that explain iching hexagrams in contemporary terms.
