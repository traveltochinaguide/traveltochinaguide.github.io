Chengdu, the capital of Sichuan province, is a city where ancient traditions blend seamlessly with modern life. It is world-famous for its Giant Panda Base and Research Center, where visitors can observe these beloved creatures in semi-natural habitats.

The city is also the gateway to some of China's most stunning natural landscapes, including the Buddha statue at Leshan and the scenic mountains of Jiuzhaigou. In the city itself, explore the historic Jinli Street, relax in People's Park, and experience the laid-back Sichuan lifestyle. Chengdu is also internationally recognized as the UNESCO City of Gastronomy -- its spicy Sichuan cuisine is legendary.

Chengdu Panda Base

The Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding (成都大熊猫繁育研究基地) is the top attraction in Chengdu. Located just 10 km from the city center, it houses over 50 giant pandas and is one of the most successful panda breeding programs in the world.

Opening hours: 07:30-18:00. Ticket: ~55 yuan. Metro: Line 3 to Panda Base Station.

Jinli Street

Jinli Street (锦里) is a famous pedestrian street known for its traditional architecture, local snacks, tea houses, and crafts. One of the oldest commercial streets in China, it comes alive in the evening with lanterns and street performances.

Dujiangyan Irrigation System

About 50 km from Chengdu, the Dujiangyan Irrigation System (都江堰) is a UNESCO World Heritage site -- an ancient water management project still in use today. An impressive feat of ancient engineering and a scenic area for hiking.

How to get there

By air: Chengdu Tianfu International Airport (TFU) has domestic and international flights. Metro, bus, or taxi to city center (45 min).

By train: High-speed trains from Beijing (~7h), Shanghai (~10h), Xi'an (~3h). Chengdu East Railway Station is closest to tourist areas.

Best time to visit

Spring (Mar-May) and autumn (Sep-Nov) offer comfortable weather. Summers are hot; winters are mild but occasionally foggy. Pandas are most active in morning feeding times.

Getting around

  • Metro: 12+ lines. Use the "天府通" app or transit card.
  • Didi: Very convenient in Chengdu.
  • Bicycle: Shared bikes (Hello, Meituan) available throughout.

Suggested itineraries

1 day: Panda Base (morning) -- Jinli Street (afternoon) -- Sichuan Opera in the evening.

3 days: Day 1: Panda Base + Jinli Street; Day 2: Dujiangyan day trip; Day 3: People's Park + Wide and Narrow Alley + Sichuan cuisine.

Practical tips

  • Sichuan food: Try mapo tofu, kung pao chicken, dan dan noodles, and Sichuan hotpot.
  • Language: Mandarin is universal; translation apps help at local restaurants.
  • Cash: Alipay/WeChat Pay dominant; carry cash for small vendors at Jinli Street.
