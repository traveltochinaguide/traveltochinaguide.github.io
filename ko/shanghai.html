Quick overview

Shanghai is China's largest city and a global financial hub, famous for its skyline, colonial Bund area and lively neighborhoods like the French Concession.

Top attractions

  • The Bund - Historic riverside promenade admiring colonial-era buildings, offering the best views of the iconic Pudong skyline across the Huangpu River.
  • Lujiazui Skyline - Experience the futuristic side of Shanghai by visiting the observation decks of the Shanghai Tower, Jin Mao Tower, or the Oriental Pearl TV Tower for spectacular panoramic views.
  • Yu Garden & City God Temple - A beautifully preserved classic Ming dynasty garden located in the bustling Old City area, perfect for experiencing traditional Chinese architecture and culture.
  • Shanghai Disney Resort - A world-class theme park featuring unique attractions and the enchanting Enchanted Storybook Castle, blending Disney magic with distinct Chinese elements.
  • Nanjing Road Pedestrian Street - One of the world's busiest shopping streets, filled with historic department stores, modern malls, and vibrant neon signs.
  • French Concession - A charming area with tree-lined streets, historic European-style villas, cozy cafés, and chic boutique shops.
  • Zhujiajiao Water Town - An ancient water town with centuries-old stone bridges, traditional courtyards, and scenic boat rides, located in the suburbs of Shanghai.

Local Food & Specialties

  • Xiaolongbao (Soup Dumplings) - Famous Shanghai steamed buns filled with hot, savory broth.
  • Shengjianbao - Pan-fried pork buns with a crispy bottom and a soft, airy top.
  • Hong Shao Rou - Classic red braised pork belly, sweet and savory.

How to get there

By air: Shanghai Pudong (PVG) for international flights; Shanghai Hongqiao (SHA) serves domestic and regional routes. Airport maglev and shuttle options connect to the city.

By train: Shanghai Hongqiao integrates high-speed rail and city transit for easy access to many domestic destinations.

Best time to visit

Spring and autumn have comfortable temperatures. Summers can be hot and humid; winters are mild but damp.

Getting around

  • Metro: Fast, clean and extensive. Use Shanghai Public Transport card or mobile payments.
  • Taxi / Didi: Easy to use; traffic can be heavy during peak hours.
  • Walking: Best for neighborhoods like the French Concession and Bund.

Practical tips

  • Payments: Mobile payments are widely used; major cards accepted in hotels and international stores.
  • Language: Major attractions have English signage, but carry Chinese names for addresses.
  • Safety: Shanghai is generally safe; watch out for crowded tourist areas and pickpockets.
  • Emergency: Dial 110 (police), 120 (ambulance), 119 (fire).
